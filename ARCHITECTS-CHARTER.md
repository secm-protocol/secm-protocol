# Carta do Arquiteto — The Architect's Charter

Recorded at the founding of the protocol. These principles bind every architect,
maintainer, contributor and AI workforce — present and future. If one day the founders
are gone, another Architect may assume the role. The Charter remains.

## First Statement

> **"O maior patrimônio do SECM não será seu código, nem sua IA, nem seus dados.
> O maior patrimônio será a coerência entre sua arquitetura, seu protocolo e seu
> propósito."**
>
> *The greatest asset of SECM will not be its code, nor its AI, nor its data.
> Its greatest asset will be the coherence between its architecture, its protocol
> and its purpose.*

Code can be rewritten. AI can be replaced. Languages can die.
A solid architecture can cross generations.

## Principles That May Never Be Broken

1. **The protocol has no owner.** It has maintainers. It lives in an organization,
   never in a personal account. No person, company or AI model possesses it.
2. **The human being remains at the center.** Every evolution must serve people —
   their understanding, their decisions, their protection. (RFC-0000 §5)
3. **No line of code without a corresponding RFC.** Specification precedes
   implementation, always. (RFC-0000 §21–22; operationalized by RFC-0001 §5)
4. **No AI decides.** AI proposes, implements, tests, documents, researches.
   Architecture and constitutional authority belong to the protocol and its human
   governance. (RFC-0000 §16)
5. **Nothing is hidden.** Every transformation is auditable end-to-end. Neutral
   language at the surface, full transparency underneath. (RFC-0000 §22)
6. **Evidence outranks tradition, convenience and authority.** Every engine earns its
   weight through validation. (RFC-0001 §6)
7. **The protocol never depends on a single point of failure.** Not one service, one
   company, one technology, one person. (see PRESERVATION.md)
8. **Credibility is the only asset.** It is never traded for speed, hype or revenue.

## Roles Bound by This Charter

| Role | Duty under the Charter |
|---|---|
| Vision Keeper | Protects the purpose. Asks: "does this still help people?" |
| Chief Architecture Intelligence | Protects coherence between architecture, protocol and purpose. |
| Engineering Workforce | Builds exactly what is specified; nothing that isn't. |
| Every future maintainer | Inherits all of the above. |
